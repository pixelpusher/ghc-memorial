Charlton, St Luke's

Memorial

Over 200 men who died in the First World War are commemorated on the memorial in Charlton on the green opposite St Luke’s Church. The memorial was unveiled in October 1920 by Brigadier-General G. H. A. White. The memorial is a stone cross of sacrifice, an upturned cross designed for the Commonwealth War Graves Commission, by Sir Reginald Blomfield.


Disclaimer: Special thanks go to Barbara Holland, Michael Leach and RGHT WW1 Project Volunteers for their hard work in researching those on the memorial. Every effort has been made to ensure accuracy, but mistakes can be made. Please get in contact with us if you spot an error or you know more these men.
